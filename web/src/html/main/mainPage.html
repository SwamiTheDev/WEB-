The World’s Top 10 Fastest Bikes in 2022

The ultimate list of the world’s top 10 fastest bikes

The superbikes mentioned below may be on your dream motorcycles wishlist. Each of these bikes are sure to increase your adrenaline rush. Not only do they look spectacular, they are the fastest bikes in the world.

Kawasaki Ninja H2R

This is not a motorcycle, it is a monster and world No 1 bike. It has a massive 998-cc, four stroke, liquid cooled - inline four, double overhead camshaft, 16 valve engine that makes 197.3 BHP @11,000 rpm. The H2R may not be the biggest bike in the world but it definitely is the quickest. It has a host of proprietary traction control and braking control systems that help keep the wheels on the road at all times. And they are required, because Kawasaki’s fastest bike does 0-100 km/h in just 2.5 seconds.

The Kawasaki Ninja H2R top speed of 400 km/h is a world record making it the fastest bike in the world today.

Suzuki Hayabusa

Another world top speed bike from Suzuki, the Hayabusa used to be on top spot before it was replaced by faster bikes. It features a 1300 cc engine with 204 BHP, and 155 Nm of torque. The Hayabusa top speed is 312 km/h with a long list of speed records thanks to its incredible engine and handling characteristics. This Suzuki motorcycle also ranks high on the top 10 racing bikes in the world. It has special twin stacked headlights, for superb visibility at high speeds, advanced ABS and Brembo disc brakes to bring this massive bike to a stop. Many riders consider this the Suzuki Hayabusa as the best bike in the world.

Honda CBR 1100 Blackbird

It seems that Honda releases newer and faster bikes every generation and the CBR 1100 Blackbird is no exception. It has a 1137 cc inline four-cylinder, liquid cooled engine that just loves to rev all the way to 10,000 rpm. The Honda’s 0-100 km/h time is a quick 2.8 seconds and it has a top speed of 305 km/h, making this one a fast bike. The maximum power it can achieve is 153 BHP and maximum torque is 109 Nm. The Blackbird sports bike name does a good job of paying homage to its stealthy characterisitcs.

Kawasaki Ninja ZX 14R

Kawasaki’s second fastest machine is the ZX 14R with a topspeed of 335 km/h and 0-100 km/h time of 2.7 seconds, making it a lightning quick and world famous bike. The engine on the ZX 14R is a 1441 cc, inline four-cylinder, liquid cooled, four valve per cylinder unit that produces 197 BHP @ 10,000 rpm. This bike is well on its way to being the fastest bike in the world given these specifications, as well as the highest cc bike in this list.

Yamaha YZF R1

Another Japanese cruiser bruiser, the Yamaha YZF R1 has a proven track record in the MotoGP championship and lets riders experience this technology in a road bike. It has a powerful 197 BHP engine that has a maximum torque of 112 Nm. The power plant is a 998 cc, four cylinder that is held in an aluminium delta box frame chassis. Riders regard the R1 as the best racing bike in the world thanks to its MotoGP heritage and winning streak. The YZF R1 has adjustable front forks, and swing arms that can be customised for the rider. It can touch a top speed of 298 km/h slotting it in the top 10 bikes in the world in terms of top speed.

BMW S1000RR

The BMW S1000 RR is one serious sports bike that is contending for fastest bike in the world. Different from the Japanese speedsters, the bike takes its DNA from its parent company BMW. The BMW S1000RR top speed of 303 km/h makes it the fastest BMW ever produced. The body is streamlined and beautiful for a bike from a European manufacturer. It comes with twin LED headlamps and LED DRLs for added safety while riding. Its engine is a 999 cc, that produces 206 BHP and 113 Nm of torque.

MV Agusta F4 RC

The MV Agusta F4 RC is a one-of-a-kind top speed bike. Made in limited numbers, this bike has a four cylinder, 205 BHP that can take the motorcycle all the way to 302 km/h. In its road going form, the bike weighs only 175 kg and sports an unmistaken Italian design from its beautiful bodywork. One of the best racing bikes to grace our roads. A true high-speed motorcycle and one of the most fastest bike in the world, the MV Agusta F4 RC has a 998 cc engine, that is completely race bred.

Aprilia RSV4

The Aprilia RSV4 is a true-blooded Italian superbike, that excites the senses with its styling, power and speed. This particular model is a track inspired road going version of the superbike complete with Ohlins front forks and a host of traction and ride control features derived from the race track. It is indeed on its way to being the world top speed bike. It has a 999cc, liquid cooled, V4 engine that produces a maximum power of 198 BHP and 115 Nm of maximum torque mated to a short shifting 6 speed transmission. It can reach a top speed of 290 km/h making it a seriously fast bike.

BMW K1300 S

This world high speed bike was released to replace the BMW K1200 S. Having been in production for some time now, this BMW has an intoxicating sound from its 1293 cc, 192 BHP, hexagonal cylinder engine. It comes meshed with a 6-speed sequential gearbox to transfer all that power to the rear wheel. With its massive engine and lightweight chassis, the BMW K1300 S can reach a top speed of 281 km/h, listed as one of the top 10 bikes in the world when it comes to maximum speed.

Aprilia RSV 1000 R Mille

This world top bike has a 998-cc, liquid cooled, electronic fuel injection engine. It is a Rotex V-twin separated by 60 degrees and comes with a anti-vibration double countershaft for smooth and refined acceleration. The bike is extremely fast and can accelerate from 0-100 km/h in 3.1 seconds. It produces 141.3 BHP @ 10,000 rpm and a maximum torque figure of 107 Nm @ 5500 rpm. It can touch a top speed of 282 km/h.
